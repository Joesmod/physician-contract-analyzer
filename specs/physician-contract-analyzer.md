## 3. Key Contract Terms to Extract

### 3.1 Compensation & Financial

| Term | What to Extract |
|------|----------------|
| **Base Salary** | Annual amount, guaranteed vs. draw, any annual increases specified |
| **Production Compensation** | Model (RVU, collections, net revenue), thresholds, tiers, rates per unit |
| **RVU Thresholds** | Base threshold, bonus tiers, wRVU vs total RVU, how RVUs are credited (personally performed vs. incident-to) |
| **Compensation Split** | Base vs. production ratio, how it shifts over time |
| **Signing Bonus** | Amount, repayment/clawback terms, triggering events, pro-ration schedule |
| **Relocation Allowance** | Amount, repayment terms |
| **Student Loan Repayment** | Amount, vesting schedule, clawback |
| **Partnership/Equity** | Buy-in terms, timeline, valuation method, vesting |

### 3.2 Benefits & Insurance

| Term | What to Extract |
|------|----------------|
| **Malpractice Insurance** | Type (occurrence vs. claims-made), coverage limits, who pays premium |
| **Tail Coverage** | Who pays, estimated cost, what triggers obligation, any caps |
| **Health/Dental/Vision** | Coverage tier, employer contribution, effective date |
| **Retirement** | 401k/403b match, profit sharing, vesting schedule |
| **CME Allowance** | Annual amount, days off for CME |
| **PTO/Vacation** | Days per year, sick leave, accrual vs. lump sum, carryover |
| **Disability Insurance** | Short-term, long-term, own-occupation definition |
| **Life Insurance** | Coverage amount, supplemental options |

### 3.3 Restrictive Covenants

| Term | What to Extract |
|------|----------------|
| **Non-Compete** | Geographic radius, duration, specialty scope, exceptions, enforceability notes by state |
| **Non-Solicitation** | Patients, employees, referral sources â€” scope and duration |
| **Non-Disparagement** | Mutual or one-way, scope |
| **Confidentiality** | Scope, duration, carve-outs |

### 3.4 Termination & Exit

| Term | What to Extract |
|------|----------------|
| **Term Length** | Initial term, renewal terms (auto-renew, mutual, evergreen) |
| **Termination With Cause** | Definition of "cause," notice period, cure period |
| **Termination Without Cause** | Notice period (each party), severance, effect on restrictive covenants |
| **Termination for Disability/Death** | Definitions, benefit continuation |
| **Post-Termination Obligations** | Tail, non-compete activation, patient transition, records |
| **Severance** | Amount, conditions, release requirements |

### 3.5 Work Requirements & Scope

| Term | What to Extract |
|------|----------------|
| **Clinical Duties** | Scope of practice, locations, patient volume expectations |
| **Call Schedule** | Frequency, compensation for call, backup arrangements |
| **Administrative Duties** | Committees, teaching, supervision expectations |
| **Moonlighting** | Permitted/prohibited, approval process, scope restrictions |
| **Intellectual Property** | Ownership of research, inventions, publications |
| **Medical Directorship** | Additional duties, compensation, separate agreement |

### 3.6 Legal & Structural

| Term | What to Extract |
|------|----------------|
| **Assignment Clause** | Can employer assign without consent? Physician's rights on change of control |
| **Change of Control** | Definition, physician's options (termination right, covenant release) |
| **Governing Law** | State, arbitration vs. litigation, venue |
| **Dispute Resolution** | Mediation, arbitration, jury waiver |
| **Indemnification** | Scope, mutual or one-way |
| **Amendment Process** | Written mutual consent, or employer unilateral changes allowed |

## 4. Red Flag Alerts â€” Sample Output

These appear as prominent warning boxes in the report.

---

> ### ğŸš© RED FLAG: Tail Coverage Trap â€” You Could Owe $30,000â€“$80,000 When You Leave
>
> **Section 8.3 â€” Malpractice Insurance**
>
> This contract uses **claims-made** malpractice insurance and assigns **100% of tail coverage costs to you** upon separation â€” regardless of whether you quit, are terminated without cause, or the employer simply chooses not to renew. Tail premiums for your specialty typically run **$30,000â€“$80,000** and are due as a lump sum. This is money you'll owe even if the employer fires you with 60 days' notice. Many physicians don't discover this cost until it's too late. **In market-standard contracts, the employer covers tail if the physician completes the initial term or is terminated without cause.** This clause effectively functions as a golden handcuff â€” leaving at any point triggers a five-figure bill. **Ask the employer to cover tail upon completion of the initial term or upon without-cause termination, at minimum.**

---

> ### ğŸš© RED FLAG: Non-Compete Would Force Relocation â€” 30 Miles, 24 Months, All Subspecialties
>
> **Section 12.1 â€” Restrictive Covenants**
>
> The non-compete clause restricts you from practicing **any form of cardiology** within a **30-mile radius** of **any employer location** for **24 months** after separation. The employer operates 14 locations across the metro area. When mapped, this non-compete effectively **blankets the entire metropolitan region and surrounding suburbs**. If enforced, you would need to either relocate your family or stop practicing your specialty for two years. The national median non-compete for cardiologists is 15 miles and 12 months. This clause is **2x the typical radius and duration** and applies even if *the employer* terminates you without cause. **This is one of the most consequential clauses in the contract. Request a reduction to 15 miles / 12 months, limit it to your specific subspecialty, and add a carve-out that voids the non-compete if you are terminated without cause.**

---

> ### ğŸš© RED FLAG: Termination Without Cause â€” Employer Can End Contract in 60 Days With No Cure Period and No Severance
>
> **Section 10.2 â€” Termination Without Cause**
>
> The employer can terminate this contract **without cause** with only **60 days' written notice**. There is **no cure period** (no chance to fix an issue before termination), **no severance payment**, and the **non-compete and tail coverage obligations activate immediately** upon termination. Meanwhile, if *you* want to leave without cause, you must provide **90 days' notice**. This asymmetry means the employer can cut you loose in 60 days, stick you with a $30Kâ€“$80K tail bill, and bar you from practicing within 30 miles for 2 years â€” while you can't leave without giving 50% more notice. **Request: (1) equal notice periods of 90 days for both parties, (2) a 30-day cure period before without-cause termination, (3) 3â€“6 months' severance if terminated without cause, and (4) release from non-compete and tail obligations upon employer-initiated without-cause termination.**
